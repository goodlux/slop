## The Biomechanics of Flow

When an octopus undulates, it's running a distributed algorithm:

1. **Hydrostatic skeleton** - Muscular hydrostats maintain constant volume while changing shape. Squeeze here, bulge there. The math of incompressible fluids made flesh.

2. **Arm autonomy** - Each arm contains ~40 million neurons. They don't wait for central command. They *improvise*.

3. **Wave propagation** - Movements ripple from base to tip at variable frequencies. Multiple waves can travel simultaneously along a single arm.

4. **Chromatic accompaniment** - Chromatophores fire in sync with movement, creating traveling waves of color that mirror the physical undulation.
